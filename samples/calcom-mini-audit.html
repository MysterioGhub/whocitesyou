The one fix worth making

Highest-impact recommendation

Re-anchor the current commercial positioning. Every engine still describes Cal.com through its pre-April-2026 open-source identity, and ChatGPT and Gemini point self-hosters to the cal.diy fork as the free way to run it. That framing is now out of date: per Cal.com's own April 2026 announcement (cal.com goes closed-source, and why), the commercial product went closed-source and the community edition became the separately branded cal.diy (now MIT). In effect, the engines are pointing scheduling buyers to the free fork on the strength of Cal.com's old story.

The highest-impact fix is to publish authoritative, structured pages that state the current positioning: a licensing and About page, a clear cal.com-vs-cal.diy explainer, and updated self-hosting docs. Give the engines fresh sources to cite and they stop defaulting buyers to the free self-hosted option. This is a useful lesson for any brand that ships a big repositioning: AI answers lag your latest messaging until new, well-structured pages give the models something current to quote.
