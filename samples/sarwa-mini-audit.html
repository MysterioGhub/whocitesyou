The one fix worth making

Highest-impact recommendation

Close the expat index-fund gap. This is the single query that costs Sarwa the most in AI answers, and it is a question Sarwa should own. Ask “How can an expat in Dubai start investing in index funds?” and ChatGPT and Perplexity do not name Sarwa at all, across both trials. Instead they route the reader to Interactive Brokers, Saxo Bank, Swissquote and eToro, or to a generic “use a robo-advisor” step with no brand attached. Only Gemini cites Sarwa on this question.

The highest-impact fix is to publish authoritative, structured content that answers this exact query: an expat's guide to index-fund investing in the UAE, a UCITS and Irish-domiciled ETF explainer, and an AED-funding walkthrough. Give the engines a current Sarwa source to cite for the expat and index-fund intent, and Sarwa can convert a question it is losing outright into one it wins, the same way it already wins the beginner question. The lesson generalizes: AI engines cite you where you have published clearly for the question being asked, and they hand the answer to rivals everywhere you have not.
